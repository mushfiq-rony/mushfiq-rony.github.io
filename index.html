Background

About Me

I am a third-year PhD student in Data Science at the University of Mississippi Medical Center (2024–present), where I also completed my MS in Biostatistics and Data Science. My research centers on functional data analysis, with a particular emphasis on functional and variable domain regression methods applied to complex biomedical signals and neurocognitive outcomes.

Alongside my PhD, I work part-time as a Student Biostatistician at the MIND Center, UMMC. My work there focuses on the statistical analysis of aging and neurodegeneration, including providing statistical support in modeling plasma biomarkers of Alzheimer's disease (such as amyloid-β, p-tau, and GFAP) and applying multistate models to characterize disease progression from normal cognition through mild cognitive impairment to dementia. I have contributed to research on cerebrovascular and Alzheimer's disease biomarkers in the ARIC cohort, with a focus on cognitive and mobility impairment.

I hold a BS in Statistics from the University of Dhaka, and have prior professional experience at icddr,b and Save the Children Bangladesh, working on clinical trials and maternal health analytics.
